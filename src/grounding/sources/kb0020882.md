## Who Can Submit

Article authoring is scoped to Tier II and Tier III support groups, Subject Matter Experts, and Knowledge team members. Tier I support staff cannot author articles directly; they submit on behalf of Tier I via Tier II/III. All authors must have Can Read + Can Contribute access on the Colleague Technology knowledge base under the Workday functional code (Paul Beswick's org).

Every article must have a listed Subject Matter Expert who is contactable for updates and queries. If the SME field is left blank at submission, it defaults to the article author.

<!-- section:article-creation-steps -->
## Article Creation Steps

To create a new Technical Knowledge article:

1. Navigate to the Knowledge home page in ServiceNow.
2. Select the Colleague Technology knowledge base.
3. Click **Create an Article**.
4. Select the **Technical Knowledge** template.

The Technical Knowledge template structures the article into three HTML fields: Summary, Problem, and Resolution. Complete each field per the content requirements below. The Summary is a 1–2 sentence description of what the article covers and when it applies. The Problem field captures the specific issue, scenario, or question the article addresses. The Resolution field contains the solution and steps.

For article updates (not new creation), refer to KB0022991 for the management SOP covering checkout, edit, publish, retire, and delete lifecycle.

<!-- section:naming-convention -->
## Article Naming Convention

Article titles go in the **Short description** field. Format:

```
[Application/Topic] - [Type Descriptor] - [OPCO or Line of Business] - [Region]
```

Example: `ServiceNow - Network ID Lookup - All LoBs - Global`

Rules:
- 160 character hard limit — abbreviate if needed (e.g. "management" → "mgmt").
- Region options: EMEA, NASA, APAC, Global.
- OPCO: specific operating company name, or "All LoBs" if global.
- This naming convention intentionally mirrors the 4-part summary analysts use on incident tickets for consistency.

<!-- section:required-fields -->
## Required Fields

The following fields must be completed on every new Technical Knowledge article. Fields are classified by whether ServiceNow itself enforces them (SNOW_REQUIRED) or whether they are required by MMC Knowledge team practice (ORG_REQUESTED).

- **Knowledge base** (SNOW_REQUIRED + AUTO) — Pre-filled as "Colleague Technology". Do not change.
- **Short description** (SNOW_REQUIRED) — Article title. Must follow the 4-part naming convention. Hard limit: 160 characters.
- **Summary** (SNOW_REQUIRED) — HTML field. 1–2 sentences on what the article covers and when it applies.
- **Problem** (SNOW_REQUIRED) — HTML field. The specific issue, scenario, or question the article addresses.
- **Resolution** (SNOW_REQUIRED) — HTML field. Solution and steps. Must include Configuration Item, SME, assignment group, OPCO, region. No passwords or external download links.
- **Category** (ORG_REQUESTED) — Not enforced by ServiceNow but required by org. Mostly "Software"; own sections for Operating Systems, ServiceNow, and Procedure/SOP articles.
- **Subject matter expert** (ORG_REQUESTED) — Lookup field for named SME individual. If blank, defaults to author. Person must be contactable for updates and queries.
- **SME Group** (ORG_REQUESTED) — Team or group the SME belongs to. Required alongside individual SME.

<!-- section:resolution-field-software -->
## Resolution Field — Software (11-point)

For Software articles, the Resolution field must contain the following 11 items:

1. Name of software
2. Subject matter expert
3. Operating Company and Region (EMEA, NASA, APAC, or Global)
4. One or two sentence summary of the article topic
5. Description of the software (what it is, what it does, who uses it)
6. How it is accessed: URL, credentials, special processes
7. Potential error messages / common issues and how to address them
8. Screenshots
9. Support processes
10. The Configuration Item as listed in ServiceNow
11. Support information: ServiceNow assignment group names, email addresses, individual names for on-call/escalation

<!-- section:resolution-field-support-process -->
## Resolution Field — Support Process (7-point)

For Support Process articles, the Resolution field must contain the following 7 items:

1. Name of process
2. Subject matter expert
3. Operating Company and Region
4. One or two sentence summary
5. Relevant information including: summary of process(es), screenshots, web addresses and links to resources
6. Most relevant Configuration Item as listed in ServiceNow
7. Support information: assignment group names, email addresses, escalation contacts

<!-- section:security-rules -->
## Security Rules

Articles in the Colleague Technology knowledge base are subject to two non-negotiable security rules:

**DO NOT** include links to external downloads, or information that may be used to gain access to a workstation (e.g. passwords to specific systems).

These rules apply to both Software articles and Support Process articles. Articles that violate these rules will be rejected at approval.

<!-- section:attachments -->
## Attachments

Attachments may be added to an article via the ServiceNow form's attachment controls. Two checkboxes govern attachment display:

- **Attachment link** (OPTIONAL) — Adds an attachment link in the article header. Use only when a top-level attachment link is needed immediately.
- **Display attachments** (OPTIONAL) — Shows all attachments at the bottom of the article. Only tick if the article has documents only, no images. Mixed attachments (images and documents together) look messy — embed links in the Resolution field instead.

For images specifically, upload via the rich-text editor in the Summary, Problem, or Resolution HTML fields. Refer to KB0022991 for the full image upload and attachment process.

<!-- section:categorisation -->
## Categorisation

Use the category that best fits the content. Default is **Software** but always verify first.

Categories requiring their own dedicated section (not Software):
- Operating Systems
- ServiceNow
- Procedure / SOP-based articles
